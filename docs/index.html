Physiological Marker

Alpha Reactivity

In a healthy brain, opening the eyes immediately suppresses posterior Alpha rhythms (8–13 Hz)—a phenomenon known as alpha reactivity or desynchronization.

In Alzheimer's disease, damage to the brain's cholinergic pathways (specifically the projections extending from the nucleus basalis of Meynert) impairs this desynchronization. The brain struggles to adapt its rhythms to external stimulus, providing a distinct biomarker for classification.

5-Fold Cross-Validation Framework

To safeguard clinical integrity, our pipeline uses a strict, stratified 5-fold validation loop. This avoids artificial performance inflation.

1

Stratified Folds: Divides data while preserving the exact ratio of Alzheimer's to Control patients in each subset.

2

No Feature Leakage: Normalization parameters (StandardScaler) are computed strictly on training folds and applied to test folds, preventing data bleed.

3

Generalization Proof: Test metrics represent clinical performance on completely unseen biological brains.

Validation Summary: Stratification isolates individual patients so that the same patient's temporal samples never appear in both the training and test sets simultaneously.
